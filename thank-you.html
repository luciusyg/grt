Frequently Asked Questions

What/when can event backdrops be used for?

Backdrops are an easy way to create a focal point during your event. Product launches, trade exhibitions, roadshows, dinner & dance, conferences, a photo backdrop for your wedding guests? These are all perfect situations to use a backdrop

How much lead time will you require?

Upon confirmation a minimum of 4 working days before the setup will be needed to build and print the backdrop. If you require a shorter lead time please contact us.

How much time is needed for setup / teardown?

or an average stage backdrop a minimum of two hours is required for setup and one hour for teardown.

What is the difference between a sticker vs banner print?

Sticker print produces a clearer photographic quality print compared to banner print, however it is slightly more costly than banner printing. This also depends on the quality of the artwork provided.

What are the artwork requirements for both sticker and banners?

  • Format: PDF, Tiff, EPS
  • Colour model: CMYK
  • Safe space: Please allow 10mm border of safe space in your artwork where text of vital details should not be placed
  • Bleed: 100mm for pvc banners and a minimum of 10mm for stickers

    • Should I use gloss or matt laminate finish?

    Our recommendation will be to opt for matt laminate. Gloss laminate reflects light, as such any light shining on it or camera flash will produce a glaring effect. Matt laminate solves this by softening the glare. So for backdrops, especially those used as a photowall, matt laminate will be your ideal choice.

    Are the backdrops weather proof / suitable for outdoor usage?

    We recommend you do not place the backdrops out in the open for more than a day. As it is made from wood, long exposure to rain and sun will compromise on its structural integrity and also damage the print.

    Can I reused my backdrop?

    Smaller sized backdrops that needs no dismantling can be kept and reused. However, it will not be possible for bigger backdrops as the print will be damaged during dismantling

    When will a job be considered confirmed?

    A job is considered confirmed once you have sent back the endorsed copy of the quotation with the 50% deposit. For rush jobs the deposit must be made through a bank transfer

    What are your payment terms?

    A deposit of 50% of the total project sum will have to be paid through a bank transfer for the job to be considered confirmed. The remaining 50% payment will have to be paid on the last day during tear down
